\begin{abstract}
Topological signal processing places signals on the triangles of a simplicial
complex, but which triangles are ``filled'' is often unknown and must be
inferred from edge flows. Existing work gives algorithms; we ask when the task
is \emph{possible at all}. The latent structure is observed only through the
\emph{curl} of the flows, which annihilates gradient and harmonic nuisances;
classical detection theory then yields a curl-invisibility threshold
$\rho^\star(N)=\Theta(\sqrt{\log(1/\delta)/N})$, a $\log\binom pk$ Fano
converse for joint recovery, and exact per-triangle marginal laws with
effective SNR $\sigma_c^2/(\sigma_n^2(\bm{G}^{-1})_{\tau\tau})$ after a
GLS-type decorrelation. Our main result corrects the folklore ``rank
obstruction'': it is a \emph{first- vs.\ second-order identifiability
dichotomy}. Equal-curl-subspace supports are indistinguishable under
deterministic signals --- on $K_n$ the curl subspace carries a $3/n$
degrees-of-freedom ratio --- yet under random signals \emph{every} support is
identifiable at \emph{any} rank deficiency, because the lifted signatures
$\{\bm b_\tau\bm b_\tau^{\!\top}\}$ are always linearly independent.
Unfavorable geometry costs sample complexity, not possibility: minimal
confusers (tetrahedral face swaps) have universal curl-domain separation
$16$, giving exponent $\rho_2^2(1+o(1))$, $\rho_2=\sigma_c^2/\sigma_n^2$. All
constants are Monte-Carlo validated, and the theory recovers genuine,
unplanted structure from real data --- tropical-cyclone circulation from ERA5
wind-field edge flows, validated against full-resolution vorticity and the
independent IBTrACS archive. Code and data are released.
\end{abstract}

\begin{keywords}
Topological signal processing, simplicial complexes, Hodge decomposition,

\section{Introduction}
Graph signal processing captures pairwise structure, but many networked
measurements---currency exchange, traffic circulation, neural flow, statistical
ranking---carry \emph{higher-order} structure that lives on the triangles of a
simplicial complex \cite{barbarossa2020topological,schaub2021signal,lim2020hodge}.
Topological signal processing (TSP) has rapidly built a toolbox on the Hodge
Laplacian: convolutional filters \cite{yang2022simplicial}, maximally localized
dictionaries \cite{battiloro2023topological}, Dirac operators
\cite{calmon2023dirac}, adaptive estimation \cite{marinucci2025topological}, and
unrolled reconstruction \cite{liu2024unrolling}. A recurring primitive is that the
set of filled triangles is unknown and must be learned from edge-flow signals.

\emph{Prior work.} Learning a graph topology from node signals is well charted
\cite{dong2019learning,mateos2019connecting}. Its higher-order analogue ---
inferring which triangles are filled --- has been treated
\emph{algorithmically}: MAP estimation \cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial}, greedy
topology learning \cite{buciulea2025learning}, sparse cell-complex augmentation
\cite{hoppe2023representing}; adaptive schemes \cite{marinucci2025topological}
list structure discovery as open. HodgeRank \cite{jiang2011statistical}
established the curl as the signature of higher-order inconsistency. Missing is
the \emph{converse side}: when is the task information-theoretically solvable,
at what snapshot budget, and how does the answer depend on the geometry?
(Simplicial/hypergraph SBM detectability observes the structure
\emph{directly}; here it is latent, seen only through the flows it shapes.)
We supply that converse side. Our tools are deliberately classical ---
detection theory and Fano arguments \cite{poor1994introduction,cover2006elements},
in the lineage of support-recovery and sparse-covariance detection limits
\cite{wainwright2009information,amini2009high,berthet2013optimal,cai2010optimal}
--- our contribution is the reduction that makes them bite, plus one dichotomy
theorem (Thm.~\ref{thm:rank}) we believe is genuinely new:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A \emph{curl-annihilation} reduction (Lemma~\ref{lem:curl}; the
detection-theoretic use of HodgeRank's observation): the latent structure is
observable only through the curl, giving a standard two-variance test
(Prop.~\ref{prop:single}) and an \emph{exact} finite-sample product law over
edge-disjoint candidates (Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint}).
\item Converse thresholds through the reduction (Thm.~\ref{thm:invis}): the
sample-complexity scaling $\rho^\star(N)=\Theta(\sqrt{\log(1/\delta)/N})$ at
the exponent level, and a Fano $\log\binom pk$ factor for joint recovery.
\item \emph{Geometry-aware decorrelation} for edge-sharing candidates --- the
GLS/BLUE inversion $\hat{\bm y}=\bm G^{-1}\bm c$ \cite{kay1993fundamentals}
--- with exact marginal laws whose effective SNR is the variance-inflation
factor's (Thm.~\ref{thm:whiten}), plus a union-bound guarantee
(Cor.~\ref{cor:union}).
\item Main theorem: a \emph{first- vs.\ second-order identifiability dichotomy}
(Thm.~\ref{thm:rank}) correcting the folklore rank obstruction: equal-image
supports are indistinguishable under deterministic signals (on $K_n$: a $3/n$
DoF ratio), yet under random signals \emph{every} support is identifiable at
\emph{any} rank deficiency (lifted-spark Lemma~\ref{lem:spark}), at a price
with universal constants (swap separation $16$; exponent $\rho_2^2(1+o(1))$).
\end{enumerate}
Every result is Monte-Carlo validated. On real data we recover \emph{genuine,
unplanted} structure --- cyclone circulation from ERA5 edge flows, validated
against full-resolution vorticity and the independent IBTrACS archive ---
with FX as a machine-precision consistency check and road networks grounding
the recovery laws on real geometry.

\section{Background and signal model}
Fix a graph $(\mathcal V,\mathcal E)$ with node--edge incidence
$\B_1\in\R^{|\mathcal V|\times|\mathcal E|}$ and, for a candidate triangle set
$\Tcal$ (e.g.\ all $3$-cliques), edge--triangle incidence
$\B_2\in\R^{|\mathcal E|\times|\Tcal|}$; $N$ is the snapshot count,
$p=|\Tcal|$. The identity $\B_1\B_2=\bm 0$ holds, and the Hodge $1$-Laplacian
$\bm L_1=\B_1^{\!\top}\B_1+\B_2\B_2^{\!\top}$ induces the orthogonal split
\begin{equation}
\R^{|\mathcal E|}=\underbrace{\mathrm{im}\,\B_1^{\!\top}}_{\text{gradient}}\ \oplus\
\underbrace{\mathrm{im}\,\B_2}_{\text{curl}}\ \oplus\
\underbrace{\ker \bm L_1}_{\text{harmonic}} .
\end{equation}
The \emph{curl} of a flow is $\curl \bm f=\B_2^{\!\top}\bm f$. Each column of
$\B_2$ has three $\pm1$ entries, so $\bm G=\B_2^{\!\top}\B_2$ has
$G_{\tau\tau}=3$, $G_{\sigma\tau}\in\{0,\pm1\}$, nonzero iff $\sigma,\tau$
share an edge. The $1$-skeleton is known; the latent object is the filled set
$\Scal\subseteq\Tcal$. We observe $N$ i.i.d.\ snapshots
\begin{equation}\label{eq:model}
\bm f_t=\B_1^{\!\top}\bm a_t+\B_{2,\Scal}\,\bm y_t+\bm h_t+\bm n_t,\quad t=1,\dots,N,
\end{equation}
with $\bm a_t\!\sim\!\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_g^2\bm I)$ (gradient nuisance),
$\bm y_t\!\sim\!\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_c^2\bm I)$ on $\Scal$, harmonic
$\bm h_t\in\ker\bm L_1$, noise $\bm n_t\!\sim\!\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_n^2\bm I)$;
the goal is to recover $\Scal$. This is the generative family of the
algorithmic literature (cf.~\cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial}); we characterize
when its inverse problem is solvable.

\subsection{Curl annihilation and the single-triangle test}
\begin{lemma}[Curl annihilation]\label{lem:curl}
For any $\bm f$, the statistic $\B_2^{\!\top}\bm f$ depends only on the curl
component: $\B_2^{\!\top}(\B_1^{\!\top}\bm a)=\bm 0$ for all $\bm a$ and
$\B_2^{\!\top}\bm h=\bm 0$ for all $\bm h\in\ker\bm L_1$. Under \eqref{eq:model},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:curlstat}
\bm c_t \;=\; \B_2^{\!\top}\bm f_t \;=\; (\B_2^{\!\top}\B_{2,\Scal})\,\bm y_t+\B_2^{\!\top}\bm n_t .
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
$\B_1\B_2=\bm 0$ gives $\B_2^{\!\top}\B_1^{\!\top}=\bm0$. If $\bm L_1\bm h=\bm0$
then $0=\bm h^{\!\top}\bm L_1\bm h\ge\bm h^{\!\top}\B_2\B_2^{\!\top}\bm h
=\|\B_2^{\!\top}\bm h\|^2$, so $\B_2^{\!\top}\bm h=\bm0$.
\end{proof}
Thus the nuisances---however large---are removed exactly; all information
about $\Scal$ flows through \eqref{eq:curlstat}. For an \emph{isolated}
candidate $\tau$ (no shared edges) the scalar $c_{\tau,t}$ is zero-mean
Gaussian with variance $v_0=3\sigma_n^2$ if $\tau\notin\Scal$ and
$v_1=9\sigma_c^2+3\sigma_n^2=v_0(1+\rho)$ if $\tau\in\Scal$, where
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rho}
\rho\;\triangleq\;3\sigma_c^2/\sigma_n^2
\end{equation}
is the \emph{curl signal-to-noise ratio}.

\begin{proposition}[Two-variance detection]\label{prop:single}
Detecting an isolated $\tau$ from $N$ snapshots is a two-variance Gaussian test:
the energy $E=\sum_{t=1}^N c_{\tau,t}^2$ is sufficient, $E/v_i\sim\chi^2_N$ under
$H_i$, the minimum Bayes error is attained by thresholding $E$ and admits a
closed form via $\chi^2_N$ tails, and it decays as $\exp(-N\,C(v_0,v_1))$, where
$C(v_0,v_1)=\max_{s\in[0,1]} g(s)$ is the Gaussian Chernoff information
\cite{poor1994introduction,cover2006elements}, with $w_i=1/v_i$ and
\begin{equation}
g(s)=\tfrac12\big[\log(s w_0+(1-s)w_1)-s\log w_0-(1-s)\log w_1\big].
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Curl-invisibility threshold; joint-recovery converse]\label{thm:invis}
(a) With $\rho$ as in \eqref{eq:rho}, $C(\rho)=\rho^2/16+o(\rho^2)$ as
$\rho\to0$. Hence for target error $\delta$ and budget $N$, attaining the
target error \emph{exponent} requires
$\rho\ge\rho^\star(N)=\Theta\big(\sqrt{\log(1/\delta)/N}\big)$ for \emph{any}
estimator. (b) Recovering a uniformly random size-$k$ support among $p$
edge-disjoint candidates with error $\le\epsilon$ requires
$N \ge \big((1-\epsilon)\log\binom pk-\log 2\big)/\big(k\,\mathrm{KL}_1\big)$
with $\mathrm{KL}_1=(\rho-\log(1+\rho))/2\le\rho^2/4$: joint recovery pays a
$\log\binom pk$ factor over the single-triangle budget.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
(a) Chernoff information is the optimal Bayes error exponent for i.i.d.\
observations \cite{cover2006elements}; Taylor expansion gives $\rho^2/16$, and
$e^{-NC(\rho)}\le\delta$ forces the scaling. (b) Independent curl coordinates
give $I(\Scal;\bm c^N)\le Nk\,\mathrm{KL}(\mathcal N(0,v_1)\|\mathcal N(0,v_0))$;
Fano finishes (supplement \S S1, incl.\ a signal-agnostic variant). Honest
scope: (a) is exponent-level --- the finite-$N$ $50\%$ boundary sits an
order-one factor below the floor (same $1/\sqrt N$ law,
Fig.~\ref{fig:phase}); the scaling is the classical variance-detection rate
\cite{poor1994introduction,wainwright2009information}; specific to our
problem are the reduction producing $\rho$ and the constants $(3,9)$.
\end{proof}

When the candidates are pairwise edge-disjoint, the coordinates of $\bm c_t$
are \emph{independent} across triangles, so support recovery decouples
exactly:

\begin{proposition}[Exact finite-sample recovery law]\label{prop:disjoint}
For edge-disjoint candidates and the per-triangle Bayes-optimal energy threshold,
$P(\hat\Scal=\Scal)=
\prod_{\tau\in\Scal}(1-P_{\mathrm{miss}})\prod_{\tau\notin\Scal}(1-P_{\mathrm{fa}})$,
with $P_{\mathrm{miss}},P_{\mathrm{fa}}$ given by $\chi^2_N$ tails at the common
threshold. Its $50\%$ contour in the $(N,\rho)$ plane is the empirical
recovery boundary (Fig.~\ref{fig:phase}).
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Confusable triangles: geometry-aware decorrelation}
Edge-sharing candidates are \emph{confusable}: an active triangle leaks curl
energy onto neighbours through the off-diagonal entries of $\bm G$, so naive
curl-energy thresholding fails (Fig.~\ref{fig:conf}, left) and \emph{worsens}
as $\rho$ grows. The repair is the textbook GLS/BLUE inversion of the curl map
\cite{kay1993fundamentals} (for invertible $\bm G$, OLS, GLS, BLUE and ML all
coincide at $\bm G^{-1}\bm c$); what matters here is the \emph{exact
finite-sample marginal detection law} it induces, with a geometry factor that
is precisely the classical variance-inflation factor.

\begin{theorem}[Decorrelated marginal laws]\label{thm:whiten}
Assume $\B_2$ has full column rank, so $\bm G$ is invertible. Let
$\hat{\bm y}_t=\bm G^{-1}\bm c_t$ and let $\tilde{\bm y}_t$ denote $\bm y_t$
zero-padded to all of $\Tcal$. Then, exactly,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:whiten}
\hat{\bm y}_t=\tilde{\bm y}_t+\bm e_t,\qquad
\bm e_t\sim\mathcal N\big(\bm 0,\ \sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1}\big).
\end{equation}
Hence the \emph{marginal} law of each coordinate $\hat y_{\tau,t}$ is a
two-variance test with $v_{0,\tau}=\sigma_n^2(\bm G^{-1})_{\tau\tau}$,
$v_{1,\tau}=\sigma_c^2+v_{0,\tau}$, and per-triangle effective SNR
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rhoeff}
\rho^{\mathrm{eff}}_\tau=\frac{\sigma_c^2}{\sigma_n^2(\bm G^{-1})_{\tau\tau}} .
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:curl}, $\bm c_t=\bm G\tilde{\bm y}_t+\B_2^{\!\top}\bm n_t$
(the columns of $\bm G$ indexed by $\Scal$ act on $\bm y_t$; zero-padding absorbs
the indexing). Applying $\bm G^{-1}$ gives the mean exactly; the noise covariance
is $\sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1}\bm G\bm G^{-1}=\sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1}$. Gaussian marginals
of \eqref{eq:whiten} give the per-coordinate two-variance laws.
\end{proof}
$\bm G^{-1}$ decorrelates the \emph{mean} (removing leakage exactly); the
noise stays correlated --- $\sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1}$ is not diagonal --- hence
no ``whitening'', and the joint law does \emph{not} factorize.
$(\bm G^{-1})_{\tau\tau}$ is the price of geometry: $1/3$ when isolated
(recovering $\rho^{\mathrm{eff}}_\tau=\rho$), growing as signatures overlap.
What survives arbitrary correlations is a union bound over exact marginals:

\begin{corollary}[Rigorous recovery guarantee]\label{cor:union}
Threshold each $\sum_t\hat y_{\tau,t}^2$ at its marginal-optimal (or Bonferroni)
level and let $P_{\mathrm{err},\tau}$ be the resulting marginal error
probabilities from Theorem~\ref{thm:whiten}. Then
$\Pr[\hat\Scal=\Scal]\ \ge\ 1-\sum_{\tau\in\Tcal}P_{\mathrm{err},\tau}$,
regardless of the correlation structure of $\bm e_t$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Independence approximation]\label{rem:indep}
The product $\prod_\tau(1-P_{\mathrm{err},\tau})$ is exact when the scores are
independent (diagonal $\bm G$; Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint}) and an approximation
otherwise; on our diagonally-dominant benchmarks it tracks Monte-Carlo within
$0.03$ across the transition, while the union bound stays a valid, more
conservative floor (Fig.~\ref{fig:conf}, right).
\end{remark}

When curl signatures are linearly dependent ($\bm G$ singular --- e.g.\ all
$\binom n3$ triangles of $K_n$), the folklore answer --- ``identifiable only
modulo $\ker\B_2$'' --- is correct for \emph{deterministic} signals but
\emph{false} for the random model \eqref{eq:model}: the covariance carries
$\sigma_c^2\sum_{\tau\in\Scal}\bm b_\tau\bm b_\tau^{\!\top}$, strictly finer
than the column space. The correct statement is a dichotomy, resting on a
spark property of the \emph{lifted} signatures:

\begin{lemma}[Lifted spark]\label{lem:spark}
For any candidate set of distinct $3$-cliques of a simple graph, the matrices
$\{\bm b_\tau\bm b_\tau^{\!\top}\}_{\tau\in\Tcal}$ are linearly independent.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
A pair of distinct edges lies in at most one common triangle, so for
$e\ne e'$ both in $\tau$, the $(e,e')$ entry of
$\sum_\tau w_\tau\bm b_\tau\bm b_\tau^{\!\top}$ equals $\pm w_\tau$: every
coefficient is read off a distinct matrix entry.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[First- vs.\ second-order identifiability]\label{thm:rank}
Let $r=\rank\B_2$ and write $\rho_2=\sigma_c^2/\sigma_n^2$.
\emph{(i) First order.} If $\mathrm{im}\,\B_{2,\Scal}=\mathrm{im}\,\B_{2,\Scal'}$,
then under \emph{unknown deterministic} triangle signals the two supports induce
identical families of flow distributions --- indistinguishable at any SNR and
any $N$. Such confusers exist whenever the candidate set contains all four faces
of a tetrahedron ($\bm b_{012}-\bm b_{013}+\bm b_{023}-\bm b_{123}=\bm 0$); on
$K_n$, $\dim\mathrm{im}\,\B_2=\binom{n-1}{2}$, a $3/n$ DoF ratio.
\emph{(ii) Second order.} Under the random model \eqref{eq:model}, the map
$\Scal\mapsto\mathrm{Cov}(\bm f)$ is injective (Lemma~\ref{lem:spark}), so
\emph{every} support is identifiable from i.i.d.\ snapshots --- at any rank
deficiency, including all of $K_n$.
\emph{(iii) The price.} For a minimal confuser pair (a tetrahedral face swap
$\Scal'=\Scal\setminus\{\tau_a\}\cup\{\tau_b\}$), the optimal test between the
two curl-coordinate covariances has Chernoff exponent
$C_G=\rho_2^2\,(1+o(1))$ as $\rho_2\to0$, since the curl-domain separation is
\emph{universal}:
$\|\bm u_a\bm u_a^{\!\top}-\bm u_b\bm u_b^{\!\top}\|_F^2
= 9+9-2(\bm u_a^{\!\top}\bm u_b)^2 = 16$
(faces share exactly one edge), and the Bhattacharyya expansion gives
$C_G=(\rho_2^2/16)\cdot16\,(1+o(1))$. Hence distinguishing the pair costs
$N^\star\sim\log(1/\delta)/\rho_2^2$ snapshots, and a Fano argument over the
$4\binom n4$ leave-one-face-out hypotheses adds a $\log n$ factor for full
support recovery on $K_n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
(i) Equal images make the deterministic mean families coincide. (ii) In curl
coordinates $\bm z_t=\bm Q^{\!\top}\bm f_t$ ($\bm Q$ orthonormal basis of
$\mathrm{im}\,\B_2$) the law is $\mathcal N(\bm 0,\ \sigma_n^2\bm I_r
+\sigma_c^2\sum_{\tau\in\Scal}\bm u_\tau\bm u_\tau^{\!\top})$,
$\bm u_\tau=\bm Q^{\!\top}\bm b_\tau$; injectivity transfers from
Lemma~\ref{lem:spark} since $\bm b_\tau\in\mathrm{im}\,\B_2$. (iii) Direct
computation plus the second-order (Bhattacharyya) expansion of the Chernoff
information; constants Monte-Carlo-validated in the released tests
($C_G/\rho_2^2\to1$ to $0.998$; separation $16$ exact, exhaustively minimal
over all equal-image pairs on $K_5$). Full proofs: supplement \S S4.
\end{proof}

Theorem~\ref{thm:rank} reconciles theory with practice: second-order
estimators --- fitting the empirical curl covariance with the atoms
$\{\bm g_\tau\bm g_\tau^{\!\top}\}$, greedily or by non-negative lasso --- are
the achievers of regime (ii), verified on rank-deficient $K_5$. The $3/n$
ratio survives as \emph{first-order} observability; unfavorable geometry is a
quantifiable snapshot premium, not a wall.

\begin{remark}[Open]\label{rem:sparse}
Sharp \emph{first-order} sparse-support thresholds over $\ker\B_2$
(spark/coherence conditions) and the exact constant in (iii)'s $\log n$
factor remain open.
\end{remark}

\section{Experiments}
All experiments run on CPU from fixed seeds; every theoretical curve is overlaid
on independent Monte-Carlo simulation, and a released test-suite checks each
closed form against simulation. Code and data reproduce all figures via a single
script.

\subsection{Recovery threshold (validation of Thm.~\ref{thm:invis}, Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint})}
Fig.~\ref{fig:phase} sweeps $(N,\rho)$ on an edge-disjoint planted complex
($p=8$, $4$ active, gradient/harmonic nuisance present). Recovery transitions
rapidly (the steepness is the finite-$p$ product of sigmoids --- no
critical-point claim); the exact product law of Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint}
tracks the empirical $50\%$ contour to a median ratio $1.01$; the dotted
Bonferroni-corrected Chernoff floor ($\delta\to\delta/p$) shows the
$1/\sqrt N$ law.


\subsection{Confusability (validation of Thm.~\ref{thm:whiten}, Cor.~\ref{cor:union})}
On an edge-sharing strip ($p=9$, alternating $4$ active, $\rho=8$; full
column rank), the naive energy detector plateaus at Hamming error
${\approx}2.6$ regardless of $N$, while the decorrelated detector of
Theorem~\ref{thm:whiten} recovers exactly (Fig.~\ref{fig:conf}, left); its
recovery probability tracks the independence approximation within Monte-Carlo
error and respects the union bound, conservative in the transition as a bound
should be (right). A greedy residual-fitting baseline (standing in for
\cite{buciulea2025learning}) stalls at ${\approx}0.3$.


\subsection{Second-order identifiability where subspaces fail (Thm.~\ref{thm:rank})}
On $K_5$ ($p=10$, $\rank\B_2=6$: where a subspace argument forbids recovery),
the greedy covariance-atom estimator recovers a planted $4$-triangle support
exactly ($N=1200$, gradient nuisance present); exhaustively, all equal-image
support pairs of $K_4$ have distinct covariances and every tetrahedral swap
has separation exactly $16$. The Monte-Carlo error of the optimal
confuser-pair test respects the $e^{-NC_G}$ envelope with empirical exponent
converging onto $C_G$, and $C_G/\rho_2^2\in[0.95,1.0]$ for $\rho_2\le0.01$
(released tests).

\subsection{Real, unplanted recovery: cyclones from wind-field edge flows}
The achievability side is exercised on \emph{genuine} latent structure
(Fig.~\ref{fig:cyclone}): tropical-cyclone circulation in ERA5 winds
\cite{hersbach2020era5}, Western North Pacific, Aug--Sep 2020. Ten-meter winds
($0.7^\circ$ grid) become edge flows on a triangulated mesh (line integrals);
by Stokes' theorem a triangle's curl is its circulation --- area-integrated
vorticity --- so high-curl triangles are real atmospheric vortices. The mesh
is simply connected, so $\B_2$ has full column rank by Euler's formula: no
confusers, the favorable regime of Theorem~\ref{thm:rank}. Per 4-day window
(16 six-hourly snapshots) the temporally centered curl-\emph{energy} statistic
of Prop.~\ref{prop:single}, area-normalized to the vorticity scale, scores
each triangle; \emph{nothing is planted, no oracle parameters} (the
decorrelated variant is reported in the repository: its $\bm G^{-1}$ noise
amplification hurts pure detection ranking on this near-regular mesh).
Validation is against two independent references: full-resolution
finite-difference vorticity (never seen by the mesh detector) and the IBTrACS
best-track archive \cite{knapp2010ibtracs}, agency-verified positions
independent of the reanalysis (panel B). Degrading the snapshot budget traces
the predicted $1/\sqrt N$ scaling (panel C).

\subsection{Geometry on real infrastructure; FX as a consistency check}
Fig.~\ref{fig:traffic} grounds the recovery laws on real road geometry (TNTP
\cite{transportationnetworks}). (A)~All three networks have full-column-rank
$\B_2$ (DoF ratio $1$ vs.\ $3/n$ on $K_9$); the real equilibrium flows carry
genuine rotational energy ($2.4\%$/$4.6\%$ curl). (B)~Anaheim is
edge-disjoint, so $\bm G=3\bm I$ and planted recovery \emph{collapses to the
product law} (Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint}); the real constant background is
removed exactly by centering (df $N{-}1$) and the nuisances are annihilated,
so --- stated plainly --- the surviving statistical problem is synthetic; the
honest real recovery evidence is Fig.~\ref{fig:cyclone}. (C)~EMA exercises
Theorem~\ref{thm:whiten} on real geometry: $10$ edge-sharing pairs make
$\bm G$ non-diagonal; empirical recovery tracks the independence
approximation and respects the union bound. \emph{FX consistency check (no
figure):} edge flows from $257$ days of ECB rates on $K_9$ are by
construction a single-potential gradient $\bm f=\B_1^{\!\top}\bm p$, so
Lemma~\ref{lem:curl} predicts \emph{identically zero} curl; measured
curl/gradient $=1.8\times10^{-31}$, floating-point residue. A consistency
check on real market data (HodgeRank's arbitrage-free reading
\cite{jiang2011statistical}), not a market discovery; $K_9$'s $28/84$ rank is
panel~(A)'s red bar.

\section{Conclusion}
We characterized when higher-order structure is identifiable from edge flows:
a curl-invisibility threshold with a $\log\binom pk$ joint converse; exact
marginal laws after GLS-type decorrelation with a union-bound guarantee; and
a first- vs.\ second-order dichotomy --- rank deficiency binds only for
deterministic signals; random signals make every support identifiable at a
quantified snapshot premium. This delimits when algorithmic TSP inference
\cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial,buciulea2025learning,hoppe2023representing}
succeeds, with achievability shown on genuine, unplanted structure (ERA5
cyclone circulation, two independent references). The repository adds a
signal-agnostic Fano variant, sub-Gaussian achievability, an exact $q^{3k}$
edge-sampling law \cite{chen2015discrete}, and plug-in estimation.
Remark~\ref{rem:sparse}'s thresholds remain open.
