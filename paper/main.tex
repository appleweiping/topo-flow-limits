\title{When Are Triangles Invisible? Fundamental Limits of\\ Higher-Order Structure Identifiability from Edge Flows}

\begin{abstract}
Topological signal processing models higher-order interactions by placing signals
on the triangles of a simplicial complex, but in many applications the set of
``filled'' triangles is unknown and must be inferred from edge-flow observations.
Existing work provides algorithms for this inference; we instead ask when it is
\emph{possible at all}. We show that the latent higher-order structure is observed
only through the \emph{curl} of the edge flows, which annihilates the gradient and
harmonic parts of the signal. This reduces per-triangle detection to a two-variance
Gaussian test whose optimal error exponent is a Gaussian Chernoff information, and
yields a sharp \emph{curl-invisibility} phase: when the curl signal-to-noise ratio
$\rho$ falls below $\rho^\star(N)=\Theta(\sqrt{\log(1/\delta)/N})$, no estimator can
recover the structure from $N$ snapshots. When candidate triangles share edges, a
geometry-aware whitening of the curl statistic yields exact per-triangle marginal
detection laws with effective SNR $\rho^{\mathrm{eff}}_\tau=\sigma_c^2/(\sigma_n^2
(\bm{G}^{-1})_{\tau\tau})$ governed by the triangle Gram matrix $\bm G$, together
with a rigorous union-bound recovery guarantee. Finally we expose a second,
SNR-independent obstruction: supports are identifiable only modulo $\ker\B_2$, and
on the complete graph $K_n$ the observable curl subspace has dimension
$\binom{n-1}{2}$ --- a $3/n$ degrees-of-freedom ratio relative to the
$\binom{n}{3}$ candidate triangle coefficients. All theorems are validated against
Monte-Carlo simulation, and both obstructions are further illustrated on real
foreign-exchange flows, whose arbitrage-free (curl-free) structure the theory
predicts. Code and data are released for full reproducibility.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Graph signal processing captures pairwise structure, but many networked
measurements---currency exchange, traffic circulation, neural flow, statistical
ranking---carry \emph{higher-order} structure that lives on the triangles of a
simplicial complex \cite{barbarossa2020topological,schaub2021signal,lim2020hodge}.
Topological signal processing (TSP) has rapidly built a toolbox on the Hodge
Laplacian: convolutional filters \cite{yang2022simplicial}, maximally localized
dictionaries \cite{battiloro2023topological}, Dirac operators
\cite{calmon2023dirac}, adaptive estimation \cite{marinucci2025topological}, and
unrolled reconstruction \cite{liu2024unrolling}. A recurring primitive is that the
set of filled triangles is unknown and must be learned from edge-flow signals.

\emph{Prior work.} Learning a graph topology from node signals is by now well
charted \cite{dong2019learning,mateos2019connecting}. Its higher-order analogue
--- inferring which triangles of a complex are filled --- has so far been treated
\emph{algorithmically}: sparse-clique-sampling MAP estimation
\cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial}, greedy simplicial topology learning
\cite{buciulea2025learning}, and sparse cell-complex augmentation
\cite{hoppe2023representing} all return an estimate of the filled set, and
adaptive schemes \cite{marinucci2025topological} list structure discovery as open.
HodgeRank \cite{jiang2011statistical} established the curl of an edge flow as the
signature of higher-order inconsistency (e.g.\ arbitrage). What is missing is the
\emph{converse side} of the problem: none of these works characterizes when the
task is information-theoretically solvable, at what snapshot budget, and how the
answer depends on the geometry of the complex. Detectability thresholds
for simplicial/hypergraph stochastic block models observe the higher-order
structure \emph{directly}; here the structure is latent and observed only through
the flows it shapes. This paper supplies the missing fundamental limits, using
classical detection and information-theoretic tools
\cite{poor1994introduction,cover2006elements}. Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A \emph{curl-annihilation} reduction (Lemma~\ref{lem:curl}): the latent
structure is observable only through the flow's curl, turning per-triangle
detection into a two-variance Gaussian test whose optimal error exponent is a
Gaussian Chernoff information (Prop.~\ref{prop:single}), and making support
recovery over edge-disjoint candidates obey an \emph{exact} finite-sample product
law (Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint}).
\item A sharp \emph{curl-invisibility} phase transition (Theorem~\ref{thm:invis}):
the structure is unrecoverable below
$\rho^\star(N)=\Theta(\sqrt{\log(1/\delta)/N})$, with $C(\rho)\sim\rho^2/16$.
\item A \emph{geometry-aware} whitened detector for edge-sharing (confusable)
candidates with exact per-triangle marginal laws and effective SNR
$\rho^{\mathrm{eff}}_\tau$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:whiten}), a rigorous union-bound
recovery guarantee (Cor.~\ref{cor:union}), and empirical dominance over naive
curl-energy thresholding.
\item An SNR-independent \emph{rank obstruction} (Theorem~\ref{thm:rank}):
supports are identifiable only modulo $\ker\B_2$; on $K_n$ the observable curl
subspace carries only a $3/n$ degrees-of-freedom ratio.
\end{enumerate}
Every result is validated against Monte-Carlo simulation, and both obstructions
are illustrated on real foreign-exchange flows.

\section{Background and signal model}
\subsection{Hodge structure and notation}
Fix a graph $(\mathcal V,\mathcal E)$ oriented so that the node--edge incidence is
$\B_1\in\R^{|\mathcal V|\times|\mathcal E|}$ and, for a chosen candidate set
$\Tcal$ of triangles (e.g.\ all $3$-cliques), the edge--triangle incidence is
$\B_2\in\R^{|\mathcal E|\times|\Tcal|}$. Throughout, $N$ denotes the number of
observed snapshots and $p=|\Tcal|$ the number of candidate triangles. The
boundary-of-boundary identity $\B_1\B_2=\bm 0$ holds, and the Hodge $1$-Laplacian
$\bm L_1=\B_1^{\!\top}\B_1+\B_2\B_2^{\!\top}$ induces the orthogonal decomposition
\begin{equation}
\R^{|\mathcal E|}=\underbrace{\mathrm{im}\,\B_1^{\!\top}}_{\text{gradient}}\ \oplus\
\underbrace{\mathrm{im}\,\B_2}_{\text{curl}}\ \oplus\
\underbrace{\ker \bm L_1}_{\text{harmonic}} .
\end{equation}
The \emph{curl} of an edge flow is $\curl \bm f=\B_2^{\!\top}\bm f$; a flow induced
by a triangle signal $\bm y$ is $\B_2\bm y$. Each column of $\B_2$ has exactly
three $\pm1$ entries, so the triangle Gram matrix $\bm G=\B_2^{\!\top}\B_2$ has
$G_{\tau\tau}=3$ and $G_{\sigma\tau}\in\{0,\pm1\}$, with $G_{\sigma\tau}\ne0$ iff
$\sigma$ and $\tau$ share an edge.

\subsection{Generative model}
The $1$-skeleton is known; the latent object is the set $\Scal\subseteq\Tcal$ of
filled triangles. We observe $N$ i.i.d.\ edge-flow snapshots
\begin{equation}\label{eq:model}
\bm f_t=\B_1^{\!\top}\bm a_t+\B_{2,\Scal}\,\bm y_t+\bm h_t+\bm n_t,\quad t=1,\dots,N,
\end{equation}
with node potentials $\bm a_t\!\sim\!\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_g^2\bm I)$ (gradient
nuisance), triangle signals $\bm y_t\!\sim\!\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_c^2\bm I)$
supported on $\Scal$, harmonic nuisance $\bm h_t\in\ker\bm L_1$, and noise
$\bm n_t\!\sim\!\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_n^2\bm I)$; $\B_{2,\Scal}$ keeps the
columns of the active triangles. The goal is to recover $\Scal$ from
$\{\bm f_t\}_{t=1}^N$. This is the generative family used by the algorithmic
literature (cf.~\cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial}); we characterize when its
inverse problem is solvable.

\section{Fundamental limits}
\subsection{Curl annihilation and the single-triangle test}
\begin{lemma}[Curl annihilation]\label{lem:curl}
For any $\bm f$, the statistic $\B_2^{\!\top}\bm f$ depends only on the curl
component: $\B_2^{\!\top}(\B_1^{\!\top}\bm a)=\bm 0$ for all $\bm a$ and
$\B_2^{\!\top}\bm h=\bm 0$ for all $\bm h\in\ker\bm L_1$. Under \eqref{eq:model},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:curlstat}
\bm c_t \;=\; \B_2^{\!\top}\bm f_t \;=\; (\B_2^{\!\top}\B_{2,\Scal})\,\bm y_t+\B_2^{\!\top}\bm n_t .
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
$\B_1\B_2=\bm 0$ gives $\B_2^{\!\top}\B_1^{\!\top}=\bm0$. If $\bm L_1\bm h=\bm0$
then $0=\bm h^{\!\top}\bm L_1\bm h\ge\bm h^{\!\top}\B_2\B_2^{\!\top}\bm h
=\|\B_2^{\!\top}\bm h\|^2$, so $\B_2^{\!\top}\bm h=\bm0$.
\end{proof}
Thus the gradient and harmonic nuisances---however large---are removed exactly by
the curl map, and only the active triangles and projected noise remain. All
information about $\Scal$ flows through \eqref{eq:curlstat}.

For an \emph{isolated} candidate $\tau$ (sharing no edge with other candidates)
the scalar $c_{\tau,t}$ is zero-mean Gaussian with variance $v_0=3\sigma_n^2$ if
$\tau\notin\Scal$ and $v_1=9\sigma_c^2+3\sigma_n^2=v_0(1+\rho)$ if
$\tau\in\Scal$, where
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rho}
\rho\;\triangleq\;3\sigma_c^2/\sigma_n^2
\end{equation}
is the \emph{curl signal-to-noise ratio}.

\begin{proposition}[Two-variance detection]\label{prop:single}
Detecting an isolated $\tau$ from $N$ snapshots is a two-variance Gaussian test:
the energy $E=\sum_{t=1}^N c_{\tau,t}^2$ is sufficient, $E/v_i\sim\chi^2_N$ under
$H_i$, the minimum Bayes error is attained by thresholding $E$ and admits a
closed form via $\chi^2_N$ tails, and it decays as $\exp(-N\,C(v_0,v_1))$, where
$C(v_0,v_1)=\max_{s\in[0,1]} g(s)$ is the Gaussian Chernoff information
\cite{poor1994introduction,cover2006elements}, with $w_i=1/v_i$ and
\begin{equation}
g(s)=\tfrac12\big[\log(s w_0+(1-s)w_1)-s\log w_0-(1-s)\log w_1\big].
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\begin{theorem}[Curl-invisibility phase]\label{thm:invis}
With $\rho$ as in \eqref{eq:rho}, $C(\rho)\to0$ as $\rho\to0$ with
$C(\rho)=\rho^2/16+o(\rho^2)$. Consequently, for target error $\delta$ and budget
$N$, reliable detection requires
$\rho\ge\rho^\star(N)=\Theta\big(\sqrt{\log(1/\delta)/N}\big)$; below
$\rho^\star$ the triangle is unrecoverable by \emph{any} estimator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
The Chernoff information is the optimal Bayes error exponent for i.i.d.\
observations, so no test beats $\exp(-NC)$; Taylor-expanding
$C(v_0,v_0(1+\rho))$ at $\rho=0$ gives $\rho^2/16$, whence
$\exp(-NC(\rho))\le\delta$ forces $\rho=\Omega(\sqrt{\log(1/\delta)/N})$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Support recovery: the edge-disjoint case}
When the candidates in $\Tcal$ are pairwise edge-disjoint, the coordinates of
$\bm c_t$ are \emph{independent} across triangles (the columns of $\B_2$ have
disjoint supports and the noise is white), so support recovery decouples exactly.

\begin{proposition}[Exact finite-sample recovery law]\label{prop:disjoint}
For edge-disjoint candidates and the per-triangle Bayes-optimal energy threshold,
the probability of exact support recovery equals
$\prod_{\tau\in\Scal}(1-P_{\mathrm{miss}})\prod_{\tau\notin\Scal}(1-P_{\mathrm{fa}})$,
with $P_{\mathrm{miss}},P_{\mathrm{fa}}$ given by $\chi^2_N$ tails at the common
threshold. Its $50\%$ contour in the $(N,\rho)$ plane is the empirical phase
boundary (Fig.~\ref{fig:phase}).
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Confusable triangles: geometry-aware whitening}
Edge-sharing candidates are \emph{confusable}: an active triangle leaks curl
energy onto neighbours through the off-diagonal entries of $\bm G$, so naive
curl-energy thresholding fails --- and \emph{worsens} as $\rho$ grows, since
stronger signals leak harder (Fig.~\ref{fig:conf}, left). Whitening repairs this.

\begin{theorem}[Whitened marginal laws]\label{thm:whiten}
Assume $\B_2$ has full column rank, so $\bm G$ is invertible. Let
$\hat{\bm y}_t=\bm G^{-1}\bm c_t$ and let $\tilde{\bm y}_t$ denote $\bm y_t$
zero-padded to all of $\Tcal$. Then, exactly,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:whiten}
\hat{\bm y}_t=\tilde{\bm y}_t+\bm e_t,\qquad
\bm e_t\sim\mathcal N\big(\bm 0,\ \sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1}\big).
\end{equation}
Hence the \emph{marginal} law of each coordinate $\hat y_{\tau,t}$ is a
two-variance test with $v_{0,\tau}=\sigma_n^2(\bm G^{-1})_{\tau\tau}$,
$v_{1,\tau}=\sigma_c^2+v_{0,\tau}$, and per-triangle effective SNR
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rhoeff}
\rho^{\mathrm{eff}}_\tau=\frac{\sigma_c^2}{\sigma_n^2(\bm G^{-1})_{\tau\tau}} .
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:curl}, $\bm c_t=\bm G\tilde{\bm y}_t+\B_2^{\!\top}\bm n_t$
(the columns of $\bm G$ indexed by $\Scal$ act on $\bm y_t$; zero-padding absorbs
the indexing). Applying $\bm G^{-1}$ gives the mean exactly; the noise covariance
is $\sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1}\bm G\bm G^{-1}=\sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1}$. Gaussian marginals
of \eqref{eq:whiten} give the per-coordinate two-variance laws.
\end{proof}
The factor $(\bm G^{-1})_{\tau\tau}$ is the price of geometry: it equals $1/3$
for an isolated triangle (recovering $\rho^{\mathrm{eff}}_\tau=\rho$ of
\eqref{eq:rho}) and grows as curl signatures overlap. The coordinates of
$\bm e_t$ are correlated whenever $\bm G^{-1}$ is not diagonal, so the joint
recovery probability does \emph{not} factorize in general; what survives arbitrary
correlations is a union bound over the exact marginals:

\begin{corollary}[Rigorous recovery guarantee]\label{cor:union}
Threshold each $\sum_t\hat y_{\tau,t}^2$ at its marginal-optimal (or Bonferroni)
level and let $P_{\mathrm{err},\tau}$ be the resulting marginal error
probabilities from Theorem~\ref{thm:whiten}. Then
$\Pr[\hat\Scal=\Scal]\ \ge\ 1-\sum_{\tau\in\Tcal}P_{\mathrm{err},\tau}$,
regardless of the correlation structure of $\bm e_t$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Independence approximation]\label{rem:indep}
The product $\prod_\tau(1-P_{\mathrm{err},\tau})$ is exact for edge-disjoint
candidates (Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint}) and is otherwise an approximation. In our
benchmarks $\bm G$ is diagonally dominant, the off-diagonal correlations of
$\bm G^{-1}$ are weak, and the product law, the union bound, and Monte-Carlo
coincide within $0.025$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:conf}, right).
\end{remark}

\subsection{The rank obstruction}
Whitening cannot help when curl signatures are linearly dependent, i.e.\ when
$\bm G$ is singular.

\begin{theorem}[Geometry-side degrees-of-freedom obstruction]\label{thm:rank}
If $\mathrm{im}\,\B_{2,\Scal}=\mathrm{im}\,\B_{2,\Scal'}$ then $\Scal$ and
$\Scal'$ induce identical flow distributions and are indistinguishable at any SNR
and any $N$: without further priors, supports are identifiable only modulo
$\ker\B_2$. On the complete graph $K_n$ with all $\binom n3$ triangles as
candidates, $\dim\mathrm{im}\,\B_2=\binom{n-1}{2}$, so the ratio of observable
curl degrees of freedom to candidate triangle coefficients is exactly $3/n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Only $\mathrm{im}\,\B_{2,\Scal}$ enters the distribution of \eqref{eq:model},
giving the indistinguishability claim. For $K_n$ the $2$-skeleton is simply
connected, so $\mathrm{im}\,\B_2=\ker\B_1$, the cycle space, of dimension
$|\mathcal E|-(n-1)=\binom{n-1}{2}$; dividing by $\binom n3$ gives $3/n$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Sparsity can restore identifiability]\label{rem:sparse}
Theorem~\ref{thm:rank} concerns \emph{arbitrary} supports. Under a sparsity
prior, specific supports with $|\Scal|$ small may remain identifiable even when
$p>\rank\B_2$, provided the participating columns satisfy compressed-sensing-type
(spark/coherence) conditions; characterizing those sparse thresholds is an open
problem beyond this paper's scope.
\end{remark}

\section{Experiments}
All experiments run on CPU from fixed seeds; every theoretical curve is overlaid
on independent Monte-Carlo simulation, and a released test-suite checks each
closed form against simulation. Code and data reproduce all figures via a single
script.

\subsection{Phase transition (validation of Thm.~\ref{thm:invis}, Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint})}
Fig.~\ref{fig:phase} sweeps $(N,\rho)$ on an edge-disjoint planted complex
($p=8$, $4$ active) with gradient and harmonic nuisance present. The empirical
exact-recovery probability undergoes a sharp transition; the solid curve is the
exact product law of Prop.~\ref{prop:disjoint}, whose empirical $50\%$ contour it
tracks to a median ratio of $1.01$ across the grid. The dotted curve is the
asymptotic floor $\rho^\star\!\sim\!1/\sqrt{N}$ of Theorem~\ref{thm:invis}.

\subsection{Confusability (validation of Thm.~\ref{thm:whiten}, Cor.~\ref{cor:union})}
On an edge-sharing triangle strip ($p=9$, alternating $4$ active, $\rho=8$;
$\B_2$ has full column rank), the naive curl-energy detector plateaus at a mean
Hamming error of ${\approx}2.6$ and does not improve with $N$, while the whitened
detector of Theorem~\ref{thm:whiten} recovers the support exactly
(Fig.~\ref{fig:conf}, left). Its empirical exact-recovery probability lies
between the rigorous union bound (Cor.~\ref{cor:union}) and the independence
approximation (Remark~\ref{rem:indep}), all three agreeing within $0.025$
(Fig.~\ref{fig:conf}, right). A greedy residual-fitting baseline (standing in for
greedy topology learning \cite{buciulea2025learning}) stalls at ${\approx}0.3$.

\subsection{Illustration on real foreign-exchange flows}
We form edge flows from $257$ trading days of ECB daily reference rates over nine
currencies (complete graph $K_9$; Fig.~\ref{fig:fx}). This study \emph{illustrates
the obstructions} --- it is not a recovery benchmark, since real FX carries no
ground-truth triangle support. (A)~An arbitrage-free market is a pure gradient
flow \cite{jiang2011statistical}: measured curl energy is $10^{-31}$ of gradient
energy --- machine zero --- so higher-order structure is invisible at any budget,
exactly as Theorem~\ref{thm:invis} predicts for $\rho\to0$. (B)~The measured curl
rank on $K_9$ is $28$ against $84$ candidate coefficients, matching the $3/n$
degrees-of-freedom law of Theorem~\ref{thm:rank} (verified numerically for
$K_5$--$K_{12}$).

\section{Conclusion}
We characterized when higher-order structure can be identified from edge flows:
a curl-invisibility phase transition in the curl-SNR, exact per-triangle marginal
laws with a geometry-aware effective SNR after whitening, a rigorous union-bound
recovery guarantee, and a rank obstruction that no SNR can overcome. The results
delimit when algorithmic TSP inference
\cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial,buciulea2025learning,hoppe2023representing} can
succeed, and both obstructions bind on real data. Open directions include
sub-Gaussian converses, identifiability under partial edge sampling
\cite{chen2015discrete}, plug-in estimation of the variance parameters, and sharp
sparse-support thresholds (Remark~\ref{rem:sparse}).
