\begin{abstract}
Which triangles of a network carry higher-order interactions? Edge flows
reveal the latent filled-triangle set $\Scal$ only through their curl, and
we show that \emph{what} is identifiable is governed by the excitation
covariance $\Gam_\Scal$ of the triangle signals, through three regimes. For
positive-diagonal $\Gam_\Scal$ (known or unknown), the weighted support is
identifiable at \emph{any} rank deficiency of $\B_2$, because the lifted
signatures $\{\bm b_\tau\bm b_\tau^{\!\top}\}$ are always linearly
independent. For unrestricted PSD $\Gam_\Scal$, the population curl covariance
determines $\bm M=\bm\Sigma_z-\sigma_n^2\bm I$ exactly but pins the support
only up to $\{\Scal:\im\bm M\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal\}$ (the achievable set is
$\Ccal_\Scal=\{\bm M\!\succeq\!0:\im\bm M\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal\}$); a strictly
positive-\emph{diagonal} but correlated $\Gam_\Scal$ already defeats
identification (a rank-one witness on $K_4$). At the projector excitation
$\Gam_\Scal=\sigma_c^2(\B_{2,\Scal}^{\!\top}\B_{2,\Scal})^{+}$ the covariance
collapses to $\sigma_n^2\bm I+\sigma_c^2\bm P_{\im}$ exactly, so equal-image
supports are indistinguishable at every SNR and sample size (noise level
known throughout). This projector is exactly the Hodge-smoothness prior
used in prior topology learning \cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial}. In the diagonal
regime we give a lifted-covariance non-negative least-squares estimator that
is consistent with a fully explicit $O(1/N)$ failure bound, validated on
$K_4$--$K_8$ against subspace and greedy baselines, including an excitation
interpolation under which equal-image distinguishability provably vanishes.
On real data, the same curl statistic \emph{localizes} genuine unplanted
rotational structure --- tropical cyclones in ERA5 winds --- against a
same-field vorticity reference and an external IBTrACS archive. Code,
data, and a 52-test guardrail suite (the quoted constants checked against Monte-Carlo) are released.
\end{abstract}

\begin{keywords}
Topological signal processing, simplicial complexes, identifiability,
Hodge decomposition, covariance estimation, edge flows.
\end{keywords}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
Edge flows --- currency exchange, traffic, ranking comparisons --- carry
higher-order structure on the triangles of a simplicial complex
\cite{barbarossa2020topological,schaub2021signal,lim2020hodge}. Topological
signal processing builds filters and detectors on the Hodge Laplacian
\cite{yang2022simplicial,battiloro2023topological,calmon2023dirac,%
marinucci2025topological,liu2024unrolling}, and a recurring primitive is that
the set $\Scal$ of ``filled'' triangles is unknown. One line \emph{estimates}
$\Scal$ algorithmically (MAP under a Hodge-smoothness prior
\cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial}, greedy learning \cite{buciulea2025learning},
sparse cell-complex augmentation \cite{hoppe2023representing}); a second-order
line models the edge covariance (PCA/total-variation on the sample covariance
\cite[\S VII]{barbarossa2020topological}, ML fitting of a Hodge-structured
simplicial Gaussian model \cite{marinucci2025simplicial}); a third
\emph{detects} signals in a \emph{known} Hodge subspace \cite{liu2025matched}.
Each fixes a model and gives an algorithm; to our knowledge none characterizes,
as a function of the excitation class, \emph{when} the filled-triangle
structure is identifiable from the edge covariance --- the gap this paper
fills: \emph{what part of the latent structure is identifiable, and when?}

The answer is not a property of the geometry alone: it depends on the
\emph{excitation}. We model triangle signals as
$\bm y_\Scal\sim\mathcal N(\bm 0,\Gam_\Scal)$ with PSD excitation covariance
$\Gam_\Scal$ and show that the identifiable object moves with the regime of
$\Gam_\Scal$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:tri}): from the full weighted support
(positive diagonal, any rank deficiency), to only the achievable-covariance
set $\Ccal_\Scal$ --- so the support merely up to
$\{\Scal:\im\bm M\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal\}$ --- under unrestricted PSD
excitation, to provable
indistinguishability of equal-image supports at the projector excitation. The
last case is not a corner curiosity: the Hodge-smoothness prior of
\cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial}, $\bm y_\Scal\sim\mathcal N(\bm 0,\bm L_2^{+})$,
$\bm L_2=\B_2^{\!\top}\B_2$, \emph{is} this projector excitation --- equal-image
supports then have identical marginalized covariance likelihood, so any choice
between them comes from the prior, not the data. We thus correct a folklore
``rank obstruction'' and delimit what covariance- and image-level methods
\cite{barbarossa2020topological,marinucci2025simplicial,liu2025matched} can
resolve. Our tools are
classical --- detection theory and Fano arguments
\cite{poor1994introduction,cover2006elements}, in the lineage of
support-recovery and sparse-covariance limits
\cite{wainwright2009information,amini2009high,berthet2013optimal,cai2010optimal}
--- applied through a reduction (curl annihilation, after HodgeRank's
curl-as-inconsistency reading \cite{jiang2011statistical}) that makes them
bite. Contributions:
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt\parskip0pt\topsep2pt
\item \emph{Theory}: the three excitation regimes (Thm.~\ref{thm:tri}), with
global analytic separation constants for the isotropic regime --- equal-image
binary supports are separated by at least $9$ (attained by adding a
tetrahedron's fourth face) and equal-cardinality supports by $16$ (swaps),
proved for \emph{every} clique complex via a Johnson-graph eigenvalue
argument --- and Chernoff exponents, for fixed graph and support as
$\rho_2\to0$, whose worst case equals one isolated-triangle detection
(Thm.~\ref{thm:price}).
\item \emph{Achievability}: a lifted-covariance NNLS estimator for the
diagonal regime, consistent with a fully explicit $O(1/N)$ failure bound
in which the constants are derived and numerically spot-checked
(Thm.~\ref{thm:nnls}); Monte-Carlo validation on $K_4$--$K_8$ with random
supports against oracle-aided subspace and greedy baselines, and an
excitation-interpolation experiment in which equal-image distinguishability
collapses as predicted (Fig.~\ref{fig:so}).
\item \emph{Real data, carefully positioned}: curl-based \emph{vortex
localization} --- not topology recovery --- of unplanted tropical-cyclone
circulation in ERA5 winds, validated against a same-field vorticity
reference and the independent IBTrACS archive, with a classical baseline and
moving-block bootstrap confidence intervals (Fig.~\ref{fig:cyclone}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Model and curl reduction}
Fix a graph $(\mathcal V,\mathcal E)$ with node--edge incidence $\B_1$ and,
for a candidate triangle set $\Tcal$ (3-cliques), edge--triangle incidence
$\B_2\in\R^{|\mathcal E|\times p}$; $\B_1\B_2=\bm 0$, and each column
$\bm b_\tau$ has three $\pm1$ entries, so $\bm G=\B_2^{\!\top}\B_2$ has
$G_{\tau\tau}=3$ and $G_{\sigma\tau}\in\{0,\pm1\}$ (nonzero iff the triangles
share an edge). We observe $N$ i.i.d.\ snapshots
\begin{equation}\label{eq:model}
\bm f_t=\B_1^{\!\top}\bm a_t+\B_{2,\Scal}\,\bm y_t+\bm h_t+\bm n_t,\qquad
t=1,\dots,N,
\end{equation}
with gradient nuisance $\bm a_t\!\sim\!\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_g^2\bm I)$,
harmonic nuisance $\bm h_t$, noise
$\bm n_t\!\sim\!\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_n^2\bm I)$, and triangle excitation
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gamma}
\bm y_t\sim\mathcal N(\bm 0,\ \Gam_\Scal),\qquad \Gam_\Scal\succeq0 ,
\end{equation}
where $\Gam_\Scal\succeq0$ is the excitation covariance; making it explicit
is the point of this paper (prior generative models are special cases of it,
\S\ref{sec:intro}). We take the harmonic nuisance to be
\emph{candidate-orthogonal}, $\bm h_t\in\ker\B_2^{\!\top}$ (orthogonal to the
curl space of the \emph{full} candidate complex); the gradient part is
annihilated unconditionally since $\B_2^{\!\top}\B_1^{\!\top}=\bm0$. Then
curl projection removes both nuisances exactly
\cite{jiang2011statistical}: in an orthonormal basis $\bm Q$ of $\im\B_2$
($r=\rank\B_2$, $\B_2$ the full candidate incidence), the sufficient
statistic $\bm z_t=\bm Q^{\!\top}\bm f_t$ is Gaussian with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sigz}
\bm\Sigma_z(\Scal,\Gam_\Scal)=\sigma_n^2\bm I_r
+\bm U_\Scal\Gam_\Scal\bm U_\Scal^{\!\top},\qquad
\bm U=\bm Q^{\!\top}\B_2 ,
\end{equation}
with $\|\bm u_\tau\|^2=3$ and $\bm u_\sigma^{\!\top}\bm u_\tau=G_{\sigma\tau}$.
Identifiability of $\Scal$ from \eqref{eq:sigz} is the object of study; for
the isolated-triangle scalar test this reduces to the classical two-variance
problem with curl-SNR $\rho=3\sigma_c^2/\sigma_n^2$
\cite{poor1994introduction} (supplement \S S1--S3 develops that line:
detection thresholds, Fano converses, decorrelated detectors, plug-in
estimation).

\begin{definition}[Observational equivalence and identifiability]\label{def:id}
All statements here are about the \emph{population} $\bm\Sigma_z$ (finite-$N$
estimation is \S\ref{sec:nnls}). Two pairs $(\Scal,\Gam_\Scal)$ and
$(\Scal',\Gam_{\Scal'})$
are \emph{observationally equivalent} if
$\bm\Sigma_z(\Scal,\Gam_\Scal)=\bm\Sigma_z(\Scal',\Gam_{\Scal'})$; a functional
of $(\Scal,\Gam_\Scal)$ is \emph{identifiable} on an excitation class
$\mathcal G$ if it is constant on each equivalence class with excitations in
$\mathcal G$. The \emph{realized range} is
$\Rcal(\Scal,\Gam_\Scal)=\im(\bm\Sigma_z-\sigma_n^2\bm I)
=\im(\bm U_\Scal\Gam_\Scal^{1/2})\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal$, always identifiable
since it is a function of $\bm\Sigma_z$ alone. If $\Gam_\Scal\succ0$ then
$\Rcal=\im\bm U_\Scal$; when $\B_{2,\Scal}$ has full column rank (so $\bm
U_\Scal$ is injective) the converse holds as well.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Lifted spark]\label{lem:spark}
For any candidate set of distinct $3$-cliques of a simple graph, the matrices
$\{\bm b_\tau\bm b_\tau^{\!\top}\}_{\tau\in\Tcal}$ are linearly independent.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
A pair of distinct edges lies in at most one common triangle, so for
$e\ne e'$ both in $\tau$, the $(e,e')$ entry of
$\sum_\sigma w_\sigma\bm b_\sigma\bm b_\sigma^{\!\top}$ equals $\pm w_\tau$:
every coefficient is read off a distinct matrix entry.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Three excitation regimes]\label{thm:tri}
Let $\sigma_n^2$ be known and write $\bm M=\bm M(\Scal,\Gam)=
\bm U_\Scal\Gam\bm U_\Scal^{\!\top}=\bm\Sigma_z-\sigma_n^2\bm I$ in
\eqref{eq:sigz}. The regimes below are overlapping constraints on
$\Gam_\Scal$, not a partition (e.g.\ $\sigma_c^2\bm I$ is both diagonal (a) and
a special full-rank PSD (b)).
\emph{(a) Positive diagonal.} If $\Gam_\Scal=\mathrm{diag}(\gamma_\tau)_{\tau\in\Scal}$
with $\gamma_\tau>0$ (known or unknown), then
$\bm M=\sum_{\tau\in\Scal}\gamma_\tau\bm u_\tau\bm u_\tau^{\!\top}$ and, by
Lemma~\ref{lem:spark}, the map $(\Scal,\Gam_\Scal)\mapsto\bm\Sigma_z$ is
injective: the weighted support is identifiable at \emph{any} rank deficiency
of $\B_2$, including all $\binom n3$ triangles of $K_n$.
\emph{(b) Arbitrary PSD.} The achievable set
$\Ccal_\Scal=\{\bm U_\Scal\Gam\bm U_\Scal^{\!\top}:\Gam\succeq0\}
=\{\bm M\succeq0:\im\bm M\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal\}$, so the \emph{population}
covariance determines $\bm M$ in full but pins the support only to
$\{\Scal:\im\bm M\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal\}$: the realized range
$\Rcal=\im\bm M\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal$ is always readable, yet the candidate
image $\im\B_{2,\Scal}$ is not (a larger-image $\Scal'$ with a suitable $\Gam$
gives the same $\bm M$; $\Gam_\Scal\succ0$ does recover $\Rcal=\im\bm U_\Scal$).
The obstruction is \emph{correlation}, not zero variance: on $K_4$ (all four
faces) with $\bm c\in\ker\bm U_\Scal$ and
$\bm v=\bm e_1-\tfrac14\bm c=(.75,.25,-.25,.25)^{\!\top}$, the
strictly-positive-diagonal $\Gam'=\bm v\bm v^{\!\top}$ gives
$\bm U_\Scal\Gam'\bm U_\Scal^{\!\top}=\bm u_1\bm u_1^{\!\top}$ --- identical to
the singleton $\{\tau_1\}$ though the images are $3$- vs.\ $1$-dimensional.
Regime (a) additionally requires $\Gam$ \emph{diagonal}.
\emph{(c) Projector excitation.} For
$\Gam_\Scal=\sigma_c^2(\B_{2,\Scal}^{\!\top}\B_{2,\Scal})^{+}$,
$\bm M=\sigma_c^2\bm P_{\im\bm U_\Scal}$ \emph{exactly}; supports with equal
curl image induce \emph{identical} snapshot distributions and are
indistinguishable at every SNR and every $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
(a) Read the weights off the lifted representation (spark). (b)
$\subseteq$ is immediate; $\supseteq$: given PSD $\bm M$ with
$\im\bm M\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal$, take
$\Gam=\bm U_\Scal^{+}\bm M(\bm U_\Scal^{+})^{\!\top}\succeq0$. (c)
$\bm A(\bm A^{\!\top}\bm A)^{+}\bm A^{\!\top}=\bm P_{\im\bm A}$ (SVD). On
$K_n$, $\dim\im\B_2=\binom{n-1}{2}$ (a $3/n$ DoF ratio), and equal-image
supports exist whenever a tetrahedron is hosted
($\bm b_{012}-\bm b_{013}+\bm b_{023}-\bm b_{123}=\bm0$). Full proofs and
numerical certificates: supplement \S S4, \texttt{tests/test\_excitation.py}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope]\label{rem:scope}
(i) Deterministic unknown signals are the rank-one degenerate boundary of
regime (b): the realized range is a single direction, so not even the image is
seen. (ii) On $K_n$ with $\sigma_n$
\emph{unknown}, regime (a) retains exactly one ambiguous direction, since
$\sum_{\tau\in\Tcal}\bm u_\tau\bm u_\tau^{\!\top}=n\bm I_r$: a uniform weight
shift trades off against the noise floor. (iii) Regime (b) delimits
image-level methods --- e.g.\ matched subspace detectors
\cite{liu2025matched} --- whose population statistics see only the realized
range $\im\bm M\subseteq\im\B_{2,\Scal}$: without excitation structure, no
method can do better.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[The price in the isotropic class]\label{thm:price}
Let $\Gam_\Scal=\sigma_c^2\bm I$, $\rho_2=\sigma_c^2/\sigma_n^2$, and write the
dimensionless signature Gram $\bm D_\Scal=\sum_{\tau\in\Scal}\bm u_\tau\bm u_\tau^{\!\top}$
(so $\bm M_\Scal=\sigma_c^2\bm D_\Scal$). For \emph{any} clique complex:
distinct binary supports with equal curl image satisfy
$\|\bm D_\Scal-\bm D_{\Scal'}\|_F^2\ge 9$, with equality exactly for
single-face differences (e.g.\ $\Scal'=\Scal\cup\{$fourth face$\}$), and
$\ge16$ under equal cardinality (tetrahedral swaps). For fixed graph and
support pair, as $\rho_2\to0$ the optimal-test Chernoff exponents are
$C_G=\rho_2^2\|\Delta\bm D\|_F^2/16\,(1+o(1))$, i.e.\
$\tfrac9{16}\rho_2^2(1+o(1))$ (subset) and $\rho_2^2(1+o(1))$ (swap) --- and
$\tfrac9{16}\rho_2^2$ \emph{equals} the isolated-triangle detection exponent
$C(\rho)=\rho^2/16$ at $\rho=3\rho_2$: the hardest equal-image decision costs
one isolated-triangle detection. Hence \emph{in this scope} (fixed pair,
isotropic excitation, leading low-SNR exponent) rank deficiency is free at the
exponent level, and only the Fano log-multiplicity of the $4\binom n4$ tetrad
hypotheses ($\asymp\log n$ on $K_n$) reflects the geometry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Separations: $\|\sum_\tau c_\tau\bm b_\tau\bm b_\tau^{\!\top}\|_F^2
=\bm c^{\!\top}(9\bm I+\bm A)\bm c$ for ternary $\bm c$, where $\bm A$ is the
share-an-edge adjacency --- an induced subgraph of the Johnson graph
$J(n,3)$, so $\lambda_{\min}(\bm A)\ge-3$ by interlacing and
$\mathrm{sep}\ge6\|\bm c\|^2$; enumerate $\|\bm c\|^2\in\{1,2\}$.
Exponents: Bhattacharyya expansion gives
$C_G=\rho_2^2\|\Delta\bm D\|_F^2/16\,(1+o(1))
=\|\Delta\bm M\|_F^2/(16\sigma_n^4)\,(1+o(1))$ (the two forms coincide).
Numerically checked: swap $C_G/\rho_2^2$ within $6\times10^{-4}$ of $1$ and
subset matching the single-triangle exponent to $3\times10^{-5}$ relative (at
$\rho_2=10^{-4},10^{-5}$); separations exhaustive over all $2^{10}$ supports
of $K_5$. Proofs: supplement \S S4.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Edge-sharing marginal laws]\label{prop:gls}
Assume $\B_2$ full column rank and $\Gam_\Scal=\sigma_c^2\bm I$. The
GLS/BLUE inversion $\hat{\bm y}_t=\bm G^{-1}\B_2^{\!\top}\bm f_t$
\cite{kay1993fundamentals} satisfies, exactly,
$\hat{\bm y}_t=\tilde{\bm y}_t+\bm e_t$ with
$\bm e_t\sim\mathcal N(\bm 0,\sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1})$: each coordinate is a
two-variance test with effective SNR
$\rho^{\mathrm{eff}}_\tau=\sigma_c^2/(\sigma_n^2(\bm G^{-1})_{\tau\tau})$
--- the classical variance-inflation factor as the price of edge-sharing ---
and exact recovery admits a correlation-robust union-bound guarantee. The
noise coordinates stay correlated ($\sigma_n^2\bm G^{-1}$ is not diagonal),
so the joint law does not factorize; benchmarks, the naive-detector failure
mode, and proofs are in supplement \S S1, S3.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proposition}[Detection and joint-recovery scalings]\label{prop:thresh}
In the isotropic class, deciding one isolated triangle is the two-variance
Gaussian test with variances $3\sigma_n^2$ vs.\
$9\sigma_c^2+3\sigma_n^2$ \cite{poor1994introduction}: attaining error
$\le\delta$ at the exponent level requires the curl-SNR scale
$\rho\ge\rho^\star(N)=\Theta(\sqrt{\log(1/\delta)/N})$
($C(\rho)=\rho^2/16+o(\rho^2)$ \cite{cover2006elements}). Recovering a
uniformly random size-$k$ support among $p$ edge-disjoint candidates with
error $\le\epsilon$ requires
$N\ge[(1-\epsilon)\log\binom pk-\log2]/(k\,\mathrm{KL}_1)$,
$\mathrm{KL}_1=(\rho-\log(1+\rho))/2$ (Fano): joint recovery pays a
$\log\binom pk$ factor. Signal-agnostic and sub-Gaussian variants, the exact
edge-disjoint product law, partial edge observation ($q^{3k}$ laws), and
plug-in calibration are developed in supplement \S S1--S3; these scalings are
classical \cite{wainwright2009information} --- what is problem-specific is the
reduction that produces $\rho$ and the constants $(3,9)$.
\end{proposition}

\section{Achievability: lifted-covariance NNLS}\label{sec:nnls}
In class (a) the theory says the weighted support is identifiable; the
matching estimator is non-negative least squares on the lifted dictionary
$\bm A=[\mathrm{vec}(\bm u_\tau\bm u_\tau^{\!\top})]_\tau$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:nnls}
\hat{\bm w}=\arg\min_{\bm w\ge0}
\Big\|\hat{\bm\Sigma}_z-\sigma_n^2\bm I-\textstyle\sum_\tau w_\tau
\bm u_\tau\bm u_\tau^{\!\top}\Big\|_F^2,
\end{equation}
with $\hat{\bm\Sigma}_z=\frac1N\sum_t\bm z_t\bm z_t^{\!\top}$,
$\hat\Scal=\{\tau:\hat w_\tau>w_{\min}/2\}$, and
$w_{\min}=\min_{\tau\in\Scal}w_\tau$.

\begin{theorem}[Consistency and explicit failure bound]\label{thm:nnls}
$\hat{\bm w}\to\bm w$ almost surely, and for every $N$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bound}
\Pr[\hat\Scal\ne\Scal]\ \le\
\frac{16\big[(\mathrm{tr}\,\bm\Sigma_z)^2+\|\bm\Sigma_z\|_F^2\big]}
{N\,\sigma_{\min}^2(\bm A)\,w_{\min}^2} .
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Three derived steps, each numerically checked: (i) cone-constrained
least squares obeys the deterministic bound
$\|\hat{\bm w}-\bm w\|_2\le
2\|\hat{\bm\Sigma}_z-\bm\Sigma_z\|_F/\sigma_{\min}(\bm A)$
--- optimality of $\hat{\bm w}$, feasibility of $\bm w$,
Cauchy--Schwarz --- with $\sigma_{\min}(\bm A)>0$ by
Lemma~\ref{lem:spark};
(ii) for Gaussian snapshots
$\mathbb E\|\hat{\bm\Sigma}_z-\bm\Sigma_z\|_F^2
=[(\mathrm{tr}\,\bm\Sigma_z)^2+\|\bm\Sigma_z\|_F^2]/N$ \emph{exactly}
(Wishart second moments); (iii) Markov at threshold $w_{\min}/2$. The bound
is conservative (Markov) --- its value is the explicit $O(1/N)$ rate with
computable constants; empirically the transition occurs roughly an order of
magnitude earlier (Fig.~\ref{fig:so}). Proof: supplement \S S4.
\end{proof}

Fig.~\ref{fig:so} validates the estimator across $K_4$--$K_8$ with
per-trial \emph{random} supports at several sparsities $k$, a full
$(N,\rho_2)$ grid with $N\in\{25,\dots,400\}$ and
$\rho_2\in\{0.25,\dots,2\}$, $200$ Monte-Carlo trials per cell, and $95\%$
Wilson intervals (\texttt{run\_second\_order.py}; every cell, including the
derived bound~\eqref{eq:bound} evaluated at that cell, is in the released
JSON). Protocol: the headline threshold is the theorem's $w_{\min}/2$ (planted
weights known); an oracle-free largest-gap rule is logged alongside but is
\emph{worse} (grid-mean $0.63$ vs.\ $0.69$, ahead in $18/260$ cells). The full
edge-domain pipeline is used; the greedy comparator is a generic
covariance-atom heuristic, and both baselines get \emph{oracle} side
information (subspace: true $k$ and $\dim\im\B_{2,\Scal}$; greedy: true
$\sigma_n$).

(A)~Among these three implementations (not tuned literature methods), NNLS is
the only one with exact recovery in \emph{every} cell --- incl.\ maximal rank
deficiency ($\rank\B_2/p=21/56$ on $K_8$) --- reaching $1$ at mid-$k$ by
$N{=}400$, $\rho_2{=}1$ ($0.99$ at the densest $K_8$ cell, $k{=}28$). The
oracle-$k$ subspace baseline can lead at small $N$ but
collapses on dependent candidates ($0.00$ at $K_8$, $k{=}28$): its
\emph{population} scores tie there (Remark~\ref{rem:scope}(iii)), and its
finite-$N$ tie-breaking rides on sample eigen-anisotropy, a side channel the
projector excitation removes entirely. Greedy stalls at sparse supports
($0.43$ on $K_5$ at $k{=}2$, $N{=}400$). (B)~Recovery improves
monotonically in $\rho_2$ uniformly across rank deficiency --- the empirical
face of Theorem~\ref{thm:tri}(a). The bound~\eqref{eq:bound} upper-bounds
every cell; being a Markov bound it is conservative by one to three orders
of magnitude (the empirical failure decays exponentially, the bound as
$1/N$). It is non-vacuous (clipped value $<1$) at only $7$ of the $260$ grid
cells --- the small-$w_{\min}$, small-$N$ corner, where it falls as low as
$0.42$ (and to ${\sim}0.75$ at dedicated test cells) with zero observed
failures; elsewhere the $O(1/N)$ rate is the guarantee. (C)~Under
$\Gam_\alpha=\rho_2[(1-\alpha)\bm I+\alpha(\B_{2,\Scal}^{\!\top}\B_{2,\Scal})^{+}]$
on the $K_5$ tetrad, with the same $w_{\min}/2$ rule (the diagonal model is
deliberately misspecified for $\alpha>0$ --- that is the point), the
analytic equal-image gap shrinks continuously and the empirical NNLS
recovery of the specific $\Scal$ collapses \emph{before} the gap reaches
zero --- thresholded estimation dies earlier than distinguishability ---
with the threshold-free endpoint at $\alpha=1$ on Theorem~\ref{thm:tri}(c):
the $m{=}4$ equal-image supports of the planted cardinality have identical
distributions, so no estimator beats chance $1/m$; the plotted $0$ is
\emph{specific}-$\Scal$ recovery, itself $\le1/m$. Identifiability is a property of the excitation, not the geometry
alone. (A matrix-free form scaling to $p{\sim}10^4$ on GPU and a non-oracle BIC
threshold are in supplement~\S S6.)

\section{Real data: curl-based vortex localization}
\begin{figure*}[!t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.94\textwidth]{real_cyclone.png}
\caption{Curl-based vortex localization on real data. (A)~Centered
curl-energy scores (vorticity scale) for the single most-active $4$-day window
(argmax IBTrACS-positive triangles; illustrative only --- all metrics pool
every window); red circles: \emph{IBTrACS-positive triangle centroids} (not raw
fix positions). (B)~ROC pooled over all $15$ windows: same-field vorticity
reference, external IBTrACS labels (with PR-AUC), and the classical
coarse-vorticity baseline. (C)~Budget degradation (mean $\pm$ sd over $12$
subsample draws; uniform centered statistic, $N\ge3$).}
\label{fig:cyclone}
\end{figure*}
We position the real-data study precisely: it is \emph{vortex localization},
not filled-triangle topology recovery --- the mesh below is simply
connected, hence full-column-rank with no equal-image confusers, and real
weather carries no latent boolean support. What it demonstrates is that the
paper's sufficient statistic localizes \emph{genuine, unplanted} rotational
structure on a real vector field. Construction: ERA5 10\,m winds
\cite{hersbach2020era5} over the Western North Pacific (Aug--Sep 2020,
$0.7^\circ$/6-hourly) are subsampled to a $2.1^\circ$ right-triangulated
mesh (836 nodes, 2389 edges, 1554 triangles; \#triangles
$=|\mathcal E|-|\mathcal V|+1$ by Euler, so $\B_2$ is full column rank);
edge flows are trapezoidal line integrals of the wind in a local
equirectangular metric. By Stokes' theorem a triangle's curl is its
circulation, so the temporally centered curl-energy score
(area$^2$-normalized to the mean-vorticity scale; no oracle parameters)
ranks cyclone-bearing triangles within 4-day windows of $16$ snapshots,
pooled over the season's $15$ windows for evaluation. Storms translate several
triangle-widths per 4-day window; centering removes the window-mean flow, so
the statistic responds to the time-varying circulation a moving vortex
deposits. Validation (Fig.~\ref{fig:cyclone}B) against a \emph{same-field
consistency reference} (finite-difference vorticity on the $3\times$-finer
source grid) gives AUC $0.914$ (moving-block bootstrap $95\%$ CI
$[0.894,0.936]$), and against the \emph{external, separately curated} IBTrACS
archive \cite{knapp2010ibtracs} (raw: $726$ fixes over $13$ storms; entering
the metric: $414$ IBTrACS-positive of $23{,}310$ triangle-windows, prevalence
$1.8\%$) AUC $0.920$ $[0.879,0.951]$, PR-AUC $0.494$ $[0.273,0.666]$, and
P@$k=0.483$. Against a classical pointwise-vorticity baseline
at the same information budget (mesh-node winds), the edge-flow statistic
is better on \emph{all three} independent external metrics (baseline: AUC
$0.898$, PR-AUC $0.360$, P@$k=0.389$) and slightly lower on the internal
reference ($0.914$ vs.\ $0.948$), which shares the baseline's functional. The decorrelated (GLS) variant of Prop.~\ref{prop:gls}
ranks worse here ($\bm G^{-1}$ amplifies noise on a near-regular mesh) and
is reported in the repository. FX consistency checks and planted road-network
studies from earlier revisions are retained in the repository and
supplement, not claimed as recovery evidence.

\smallskip\noindent\emph{Limitations.} The i.i.d.-Gaussian snapshot model is
an idealization of windowed weather; evaluation is therefore threshold-free
ranking rather than support recovery, moving-block bootstrap intervals
quantify pooling uncertainty, and the internal reference is a same-field
consistency check --- only IBTrACS is independent. The vorticity-threshold
sensitivity of the internal AUC ($0.876$--$0.959$ over
$10^{-5}$--$5\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm{s}^{-1}$) is reported in the released
JSON. Nothing here claims a new cyclone detector; the experiment grounds the
paper's sufficient statistic on genuinely unplanted structure.

\section{Conclusion}
Identifiability of latent higher-order structure from edge flows is
excitation-dependent: fully identifiable under diagonal excitation at any
rank deficiency; under unrestricted PSD excitation the population covariance
pins down only the achievable-covariance set $\Ccal_\Scal$, so the support is
determined merely up to $\{\Scal:\im\bm M\subseteq\im\bm U_\Scal\}$ (not even
the candidate image in general); and provably ambiguous at the projector
excitation --- with global analytic separation constants, a worst-case
exponent equal to one isolated-triangle detection, and a consistent NNLS
estimator carrying a fully explicit $O(1/N)$ bound.
The three regimes delimit both algorithmic structure learning
\cite{gurugubelli2024simplicial,buciulea2025learning,hoppe2023representing}
and subspace detection \cite{liu2025matched}; on real data the same
statistic localizes unplanted cyclone circulation. Open problems:
identifiability \emph{between} the diagonal and free-PSD excitation classes
(e.g.\ banded or Toeplitz $\Gam_\Scal$ --- where between reading off every
weight and seeing only the image does the transition occur?); sharp
first-order sparse thresholds over $\ker\B_2$; effective sample sizes
$N_{\mathrm{eff}}$ under temporal dependence; and tightening the Markov
factor in~\eqref{eq:bound} to match the empirical transition.
